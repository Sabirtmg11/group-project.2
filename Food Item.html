Sonam Loshar Food

Enjoy the traditional flavors of Sonam Loshar with the following food items:

Yomari:

Yomari is a traditional Nepali delicacy often associated with celebrations and festivals. It is a steamed dumpling made from rice flour dough and filled with sweetened sesame seeds, jaggery (molasses), and coconut.

Gundruk Soup:

Gundruk is a fermented leafy green vegetable, typically made from mustard greens. Gundruk soup is a flavorful broth made with fermented gundruk, providing a unique and tangy taste.

Sel Roti:

Sel Roti is a traditional Nepali rice-based doughnut or ring-shaped rice bread. It is made from a batter of rice flour, milk, and sugar, deep-fried to achieve a crispy outer layer and soft inside.

Thukpa:

Thukpa is a hearty noodle soup originating from Tibetan cuisine, widely enjoyed in the Himalayan regions. It typically includes wheat or rice noodles, vegetables, and sometimes meat, all served in a flavorful broth.

These food items are a delightful representation of Nepali cuisine, and each brings its own unique taste and cultural significance to the Sonam Loshar celebration. Guests can look forward to savoring these traditional dishes and experiencing the rich flavors of Nepali culinary heritage.

Don't miss out on the delicious traditional dishes that mark the essence of Sonam Loshar!
